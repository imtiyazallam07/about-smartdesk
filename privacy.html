Privacy Policy

Last Updated: April 28, 2026

TL;DR: SmartDesk is an open-source app. We only collect anonymous analytics to count our users. No personal data, academic records, or identification ever leaves your phone.

1. Our Commitment to Privacy

SmartDesk was built by ITER students, for ITER students. We believe that your academic records, attendance history, and daily schedules are private. We use minimal, anonymous analytics strictly to understand our user base size, without tracking individual behavior.

2. Anonymous Analytics

We do not collect any Personal Identifiable Information (PII). When you use SmartDesk, we do not know who you are, what your registration number is, or what your attendance percentages look like.

  • Anonymous Usage: We collect basic, anonymous data (such as app launches) to count how many students are using SmartDesk.
  • No Personal Tracking: We don't track specific features you use or the data you enter.
  • No Account Required: You never need to sign up or provide an email.

3. Local Storage Only

All data you enter—including your timetable, library books, tasks, and attendance—is stored strictly in your device's Local Storage (SQLite/SharedPrefs). This data is never uploaded to a server.

Because your data is stored locally, deleting the app will permanently remove all your records. We cannot recover them for you because we never had access to them.

4. Data Ownership & Export

You are the sole owner of your data. SmartDesk provides tools for you to:

  • Export: Manually backup your data to your local device storage.
  • Import: Restore data from a file you previously exported.

These actions are triggered exclusively by you. No automated backups occur.

5. App Permissions

SmartDesk may request specific permissions to function correctly:

  • Storage: To allow you to export or import your data files.
  • Camera: Only if you choose to use the QR-code timetable sharing feature.
  • Notifications: To send you the 7-3-1 day reminders for library books.

6. Open Source Transparency

The entire source code of SmartDesk is available for public audit. This transparency ensures that you can verify for yourself that no data collection scripts are hidden within the app.

7. Contact

If you have questions about this policy or the open-source nature of the project, you can reach out via the official repository or community channels.
